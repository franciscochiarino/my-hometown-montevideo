Welcome to

Montevideo

Uruguay's capital city has a lot to offer. From good food and amazing people, to quality beer and legalized marihuana.

Spots

Rambla

The Rambla of Montevideo is an avenue that goes all along the coastline of Montevideo, Uruguay, and also the longest continuous sidewalk in the world. At a length of over 22.2 uninterrupted kilometres (13.7 mi), the promenade runs along the Río de la Plata and continues down the entire coast of Montevideo.

World Trade Center

The Towers Squares, is an area of remarkable aesthetic design. It is meant to be a platform for the development of business activities, art exhibitions, dance and music performances, cultural animation, a meeting spot as well as a tourist attraction.

Plaza Virgilio

You can get one of the best views of Montevideo by going to the Plaza Virgilo. It offers the perfect combination of nature and civilization, with a great panorama of the city and the sea.

Food

Mercado del puerto

Here you will eat top quality meat and vegetables (but mostly meat). One of Uruguay's traditions is the asado, wich is basically grilld meat. If you are looking for a vegan option, I would recommend another country.

Landmarks

Plaza Independencia

Plaza Independencia (Spanish for Independence Square) is the name of Montevideo's most important plaza. It separates Ciudad Vieja from downtown Montevideo, with the Gateway of The Citadel on one side and the beginning of 18 de Julio avenue on the other.

Puerta de la Ciudadela

The Puerta de la Ciudadela is one of the few remaining parts of the wall that surrounded the oldest part of the city of Montevideo, the citadel, which was torn down in 1829. It is located by Plaza Independencia in Ciudad Vieja.

Museo Blanes

The museum displays Uruguayan art ranging from the nation's founding to the present day, including the works of Juan Manuel Blanes, Pedro Figari, Rafael Barradas, José Cúneo, and Carlos González.

Carrasco

Carrasco is a barrio (neighbourhood or district) in Montevideo, Uruguay. Located on the city's southeast coast, the barrio was originally an elegant seaside resort. However, it eventually became the city's most exclusive suburb. Carrasco is regarded as one of the most expensive barrios in Montevideo, and features a wide range of architectural styles.

Weather

Weather

The weather in Montevideo includes a long summer and a short, not that cold, winter. It is recommended to visit the city on spring, so it's not so hot nor so clod.

Festivals

Carnaval

Carnival Week is considered the annual national festival of the nation. While the Carnaval extends to all the country with important events in several cities of the interior, the main activities are made in the capital of Montevideo.

Funny Stuff

Jesus drinking Mate

Mate is a traditional South American caffeine-rich infused drink, that was first consumed by the Guaraní and also spread by the Tupí people. It is the National Beverage in Argentina and Uruguay, as also in Paraguay, the Bolivian Chaco, Southern Chile, Peru and Southern Brazil. Jesus loves it.
